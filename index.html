Frequently Asked Questions

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

For starters, most people are aware that you must replace a helmet after any crash where your head hit. The foam part of a helmet is made for one-time use, and after crushing once it is no longer as protective as it was, even if it still looks intact. Bear in mind that if the helmet did its job most people would tell you that they did not even hit their head, or did not hit their head that hard. And the thin shells on most helmets now tend to hide any dents in the foam. But if you can see marks on the shell or measure any foam crush at all, replace the helmet.

Before going on a long ride, take your bike around the block and make sure it is safe to ride.First of all, your tires are going to be deflated, it is normal after long periods of time on storage. Inflate them close to the recommended level, you can find that in the wall of the tire. Make sure your brakes work. First do it off your bike, make sure the brake pads make contact with the rim or the rotor appropriately. Then do it with while riding your bike around the block. Make sure you test it with enough speed, but do it safely.

Go With the Traffic Flow. Ride on the right in the same direction as other vehicles. Obey All Traffic Laws. A bicycle is a vehicle and you're a driver Yield to Traffic When Appropriate. Be Predictable. Stay Alert at All Times. Look Before Turning. Watch for Parked Cars.
